Chapter 1: Installation

1.1 Pre-installation Precautions
- Please have installation performed by a professional technician
- Self-installation may be dangerous and voids warranty
- Confirm electrical supply specifications before installation
- Verify the installation location complies with regulations

1.2 Indoor Unit Installation Location Selection
Suitable Installation Locations:
- Wall must be sturdy and able to support indoor unit weight (over 15 kg)
- Minimum distance from ceiling: 15 cm
- Minimum distance from side wall: 10 cm
- Adequate drainage
- Good air circulation
- Remote control signal reachable

Locations to Avoid:
- Direct sunlight
- Near heat sources (kitchen, heater)
- Areas with heavy oil fumes
- Areas exposed to moisture (bathroom)
- Locations that may disturb sleep (directly above the bed)
- Curtains or furniture blocking the air outlet

1.3 Outdoor Unit Installation Location Selection
Suitable Installation Locations:
- Well-ventilated area
- Platform or wall capable of bearing >50 kg
- Easy access for maintenance and inspection
- Easy drainage

Locations to Avoid:
- Direct sunlight (consider installing a sunshade panel)
- Water accumulation
- Areas exposed to rain
- Corrosive gas environments
- Neighbors’ windows facing directly (noise considerations)

Installation Space Requirements:
- Front clearance: at least 50 cm
- Rear clearance: at least 10 cm
- Left/Right: at least 20 cm each
- Top clearance: at least 50 cm

1.4 Refrigerant Piping Arrangement
Refrigerant Pipe Length:
- Standard piping: 3 meters (included)
- Maximum piping: 15 meters
- Additional charges apply for lengths beyond the standard

Piping Notes:
- Avoid excessive bends
- Secure every 3 meters
- Protective sleeve for wall penetrations
- Proper insulation and wrapping
- Do not damage refrigerant pipes

1.5 Drain Pipe Installation
- Drain pipe must maintain a slope steeper than 1/100
- Fixed every 50 cm
- Outlet higher than ground or drainage channel
- Do not connect directly to sewer
- Winter precautions to prevent freezing

1.6 Power Wiring
Power Specifications:
- 110V model: dedicated circuit > 15 A
- 220V model: dedicated circuit > 10 A
- Use a grounded outlet
- Wiring gauge must comply with code

Wiring Notes:
- Use a dedicated circuit (do not share with other appliances)
- Proper grounding
- Do not use extension cords
- Wiring complies with indoor electrical installations code

1.7 Commissioning
After installation, verify:
1. Refrigerant pressure is normal (tested by a professional)
2. Drainage test is normal
3. All functions operate correctly
4. No abnormal noises or vibrations
5. Remote control functions normally
6. Receive the complete warranty documents

Chapter 2: Remote Control Operation

2.1 Remote Control Basic Functions
Buttons:
- Power On/Off: start or stop operation
- Mode: switch operating mode (Cool/Dehumidify/Fan/Heat)
- Temp +/-: adjust set temperature (16-30°C)
- Fan: switch fan speed (Auto/Quiet/Low/Med/High)
- Swing: adjust vertical air outlet angle
- Swing (Auto): automatic swing of outlet blades
- Power Save: enable power-saving mode
- Quiet: enable quiet operation
- Timer: set on/off timer
- Sleep: enable sleep mode

Display Indicators:
- Snowflake: cooling mode
- Sun: heating mode
- Water Droplet: dehumidification mode
- Fan: fan mode
- Temperature Digits: set temperature
- Fan Speed Icon: current fan speed

2.2 Operating Modes Description
Cooling Mode:
- Used for hot-season cooling
- Settable temperature: 16-30°C
- Suggestion: 26-28°C for best energy efficiency
- Each 1°C increase saves about 6% energy

Heating Mode:
- Used for cold-season heating
- Settable temperature: 16-30°C
- Suggestion: 20-22°C for comfort
- Outdoor temperature below 5°C reduces efficiency

Dehumidification Mode:
- Used in rainy season or humid conditions
- Auto-sets to optimal temperature and fan speed
- Do not manually adjust temperature
- Continuous use recommended not exceeding 4 hours

Fan Mode:
- Circulates air without heating or cooling
- Can be used as a fan
- Most energy-efficient mode
- Suitable for spring and autumn

Auto Mode:
- Automatically determines operation mode
- Automatically switches between cooling/heating based on room temperature
- Maintains set temperature within ±2°C
- Most convenient option

2.3 Advanced Features
Eco Mode:
- Slightly adjust set temperature up/down
- Reduces compressor operation frequency
- Can save about 20-30%
- Comfort level slightly reduced

Quiet Mode:
- Lowers operating sound to minimum
- Fan speed auto-adjusted to a gentle breeze
- Suitable for sleeping or working
- Cooling capacity reduced by about 30%

Sleep Mode:
- Automatically adjusts temperature (±1°C per hour)
- Prevents overcooling or overheating
- Reduces noise
- Optional auto-off after 1-8 hours

Timer Function:
- Set 0.5-24 hours
- Timer On (cooling before returning home)
- Timer Off (during sleep)
- On/Off can be set simultaneously

Air Direction Control:
- 5 levels of up/down angle adjustment
- Auto outlet swing
- For cooling: recommended horizontal airflow
- For heating: recommended downward airflow

Chapter 3: Cleaning and Maintenance

3.1 Filter Cleaning (every 2 weeks)
Cleaning Steps:
1. Turn off power
2. Open the indoor unit front panel
3. Remove the filter (lift upwards)
4. Vacuum to remove dust
5. Rinse with water (water temperature < 40°C)
6. Gently brush with a soft brush
7. Dry completely before reinstall
8. Close the front panel

Notes:
- Do not use hot water (>40°C)
- Do not scrub with stiff brush
- Do not machine wash
- Do not dry or expose to sun
- If damaged, replace with a new unit

3.2 Exterior Cleaning (monthly)
Indoor Unit Exterior:
1. Turn off power
2. Wipe with a dry or slightly damp cloth
3. For stubborn stains, use neutral cleaner
4. Do not rinse with water directly
5. Wipe dry with a dry cloth

Outdoor Unit Exterior:
1. Turn off power
2. Remove debris and leaves
3. Rinse fins with water (top-down)
4. Do not use high-pressure water
5. Take care not to wet electrical components

Chapter 4: Troubleshooting

4.1 Common Problem Diagnostics

Problem: Cannot start
Possible Causes:
1. Power not connected → check plug, switch, and circuit breaker
2. Remote battery is dead → replace battery
3. Protection device activated → wait 3-5 minutes then retry
4. Power supply voltage abnormal → check power supply

Problem: Not cooling or weak cooling
Possible Causes:
1. Filter dirty → clean filter
2. Temperature setting too high → lower temperature setting
3. Doors/windows not closed → close doors/windows
4. Outdoor unit not dissipating heat well → clean outdoor unit
5. Refrigerant insufficient → contact a professional for recharge
6. Room too large for the unit → consider a larger model

Problem: Noise during operation
Possible Causes:
1. Filter not properly installed → reinstall filter
2. Outdoor unit not securely mounted → check mounting screws
3. Blades damaged → replace blades
4. Fan failure → contact service

Problem: Water leakage
Possible Causes:
1. Drain pipe blocked → clear drain
2. Drain pipe slope insufficient → adjust slope
3. Drain pipe cracked → replace drain pipe
4. Excess condensation → increase insulation

4.2 Error Codes

E1 - Indoor temperature sensor abnormal
Resolution: Turn off power for 5 minutes, then retry; if persists, contact service

E2 - Outdoor temperature sensor abnormal
Resolution: Check the outdoor unit, contact service

E3 - Compressor overload protection
Resolution: Turn off for 30 minutes, then retry; check outdoor unit cooling

E4 - System high pressure protection
Resolution: Clean outdoor unit, ensure good cooling

E5 - Indoor fan motor abnormal
Resolution: Check filter installation, contact service
