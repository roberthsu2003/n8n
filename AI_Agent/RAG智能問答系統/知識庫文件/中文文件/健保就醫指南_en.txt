National Health Insurance Medical Treatment Guide

This guide helps you understand the coverage, medical treatment process, and related rights of the National Health Insurance.

Chapter 1: Basic Understanding of NHI

1.1 Use of the NHI Card
Basic Information of the NHI Card:
- IC chip card with a photo
- Contains medical records
- Universal in all medical institutions in Taiwan
- Valid for 6 years

Please bring when seeking medical treatment:
- NHI Card
- Identification document
- Co-payment fee

Lost NHI Card:
1. Report the loss immediately (call 0800-030-598)
2. Apply for a replacement (bring ID card, photo, and a 200 NTD processing fee)
3. For temporary medical treatment, you can pay out-of-pocket and apply for a refund with the receipt.

1.2 Insured Status
Persons who should be insured:
- Citizens with household registration in Taiwan
- Foreigners who have resided in Taiwan for six months
- Foreigners working in Taiwan

Insurance Categories:
Category 1: Employees (company, firm employees)
Category 2: Members of professional unions
Category 3: Farmers and fishermen
Category 4: Military personnel, civil servants, teachers, and their dependents
Category 5: Low-income households
Category 6: Others (unemployed, retired, etc.)

Premium Calculation:
Premium = Insured Amount × Insurance Rate × Co-payment Ratio

Chapter 2: Outpatient Medical Treatment

2.1 Outpatient Medical Treatment Process
1. Choose a medical institution
   - Clinics, district hospitals, regional hospitals, medical centers
   - You can call first to inquire about outpatient hours
   - Some hospitals allow online registration

2. Registration
   - On-site registration
   - Telephone registration
   - Online registration
   - Mobile app registration

3. Pay registration fee and co-payment
   - Basic clinics: 50 NTD
   - District hospitals: 80 NTD
   - Regional hospitals: 240 NTD
   - Medical centers: 420 NTD

4. Waiting
   - Pay attention to the number display
   - Wait for your number to be called

5. Consultation
   - Present NHI card
   - Explain your condition
   - The doctor will diagnose and issue a prescription

6. Dispensing Medication
   - Get medication from the outpatient pharmacy
   - Or take the prescription to a contracted NHI pharmacy to get medication

2.2 Co-payment Explanation
Outpatient co-payment:
Clinics: 50 NTD
District hospitals: 80 NTD (360 NTD without a referral)
Regional hospitals: 240 NTD (420 NTD without a referral)
Medical centers: 420 NTD (550 NTD without a referral)

Medication co-payment:
100 NTD or less: 0 NTD
101-200 NTD: 20 NTD
201-300 NTD: 40 NTD
301-400 NTD: 60 NTD
401-500 NTD: 80 NTD
501-600 NTD: 100 NTD

Individuals exempt from co-payment:
- Patients with major illnesses/injuries (with a major illness/injury card)
- Childbirth
- Medical treatment in mountain and remote island areas
- Children under 3 years old
- Low-income households
- Veterans
- Receiving preventive healthcare services

2.3 Referral System
Purpose of referral:
- Save medical resources
- Reduce co-payment
- Establish a hierarchical medical system

Referral process:
1. Go to a basic clinic for medical treatment
2. The doctor assesses the need for a referral
3. Issue a referral slip
4. Take the referral slip to a large hospital for consultation
5. Reduce the co-payment difference

Referral benefits:
- With a referral to a medical center: co-payment 420 NTD (originally 550 NTD)
- With a referral to a regional hospital: co-payment 240 NTD (originally 420 NTD)

2.4 Continuous Prescription for Chronic Diseases
Applicable to:
Chronic disease patients with stable conditions (hypertension, diabetes, etc.)

How to use:
1. The doctor issues a 3-month continuous prescription
2. You can get a 1-month supply of medication each time
3. Get medication for 3 consecutive times
4. Need a follow-up visit before the 3rd refill

Advantages:
- Reduce the number of consultations
- Save on registration fees and co-payments
- Convenient to get medication

Notes:
- Seek immediate follow-up if your condition changes
- Cannot get medication in advance
- If lost, you need to see a doctor again to get a new prescription

Chapter 3: Emergency Medical Treatment

3.1 When to go to the emergency room
Emergency medical conditions:
- Difficulty breathing
- Unconsciousness
- Chest pain
- Massive bleeding
- Severe trauma
- Severe burns
- Acute abdominal pain
- Poisoning
- Acute allergic reaction
- High fever that does not subside (>39°C)

Situations not suitable for emergency room:
- Common cold
- Getting medication for chronic diseases
- Minor skin wounds
- Mild headache
- Insomnia
It is recommended to go to a general outpatient clinic

3.2 Emergency Triage Classification
Triage system (five levels):
Level 1 (Resuscitation): Immediate treatment
- Cardiopulmonary arrest before arrival at the hospital
- Unstable vital signs

Level 2 (Critical): Treatment within 10 minutes
- Unconsciousness
- Chest pain, difficulty breathing
- Severe trauma

Level 3 (Urgent): Treatment within 30 minutes
- High fever
- Severe vomiting and diarrhea
- Moderate trauma

Level 4 (Semi-urgent): Treatment within 60 minutes
- General trauma
- Mild abdominal pain

Level 5 (Non-urgent): Treatment within 120 minutes
- Mild symptoms
- Getting medication for chronic diseases
- Recommended to switch to an outpatient clinic

3.3 Emergency Co-payment
Triage Level 1-2: No co-payment
Triage Level 3-5:
- District hospitals: 300 NTD
- Regional hospitals: 450 NTD
- Medical centers: 650 NTD

Hospitalization required after examination: Co-payment will be refunded

3.4 Ambulance Use
119 Ambulance:
- Free of charge
- For emergency medical conditions
- Prioritizes sending to the nearest appropriate hospital

Private ambulance:
- Charges apply (approx. 600-1500 NTD)
- Can specify a hospital
- Reservation required

Note: Do not occupy 119 ambulance resources for non-emergency situations

Chapter 4: Hospitalization Related

4.1 Hospitalization Process
1. Complete hospitalization procedures
   - Fill out hospitalization consent form
   - Pay hospitalization deposit
   - Choose ward class

2. During hospitalization
   - Cooperate with medical treatment
   - Regular visiting hours
   - Ward management rules

3. Discharge and payment
   - Complete discharge procedures
   - Settle the bill
   - Receive discharge summary

4.2 Hospitalization Co-payment
Hospitalization co-payment:
- Acute ward: 5%-10% (depending on the length of stay)
- Chronic ward: 5%-20%
- No charge for readmission for the same disease within 30 days

Co-payment ceiling:
- Per hospitalization: 39,000 NTD
- Annual cumulative: 69,000 NTD

4.3 Ward Difference
Ward class:
NHI ward: Free (3-6 person room)
Two-person room difference: Approx. 500-2000 NTD/day
Single-person room difference: Approx. 1000-5000 NTD/day
ICU room difference: Approx. 3000-8000 NTD/day

Note:
- Ward difference fees are not covered by NHI
- Must be paid out-of-pocket
- You can choose an NHI ward without paying the difference

Chapter 5: NHI Coverage

5.1 Outpatient Coverage Items
Covered items:
- Consultation fee
- Laboratory test fee
- General examination
- Treatment fee
- Surgery fee
- Medication fee (NHI drugs)
- Injection fee
- Therapy fee

5.2 Inpatient Coverage Items
Covered items:
- Ward fee (NHI ward)
- Meal fee
- Nursing fee
- Laboratory test fee
- Surgery fee
- Anesthesia fee
- Medication fee (NHI drugs)
- Radiology fee
- Rehabilitation therapy fee

5.3 Non-Covered Items
Not covered by NHI:
- Cosmetic surgery
- Non-essential corrective surgery
- Preventive injections (with some exceptions)
- Health check-ups
- Ward difference
- Designated physician fee
- Registration fee
- Special material difference
- Non-NHI drugs
- Medical treatment abroad (except in special cases)

5.4 Special Material Difference
NHI only covers basic items for some special materials, and you need to pay the difference for better materials:
- Intraocular lens
- Heart stent
- Artificial joint
- Pacemaker
- Drug-eluting stent

Suggestion: Choose suitable materials based on the doctor's explanation and your own financial ability

Chapter 6: Major Illness/Injury

6.1 Scope of Major Illness/Injury
30 categories of major illnesses/injuries:
1. Cancer
2. Dialysis
3. Hemophilia
4. Myasthenia gravis
5. Organ transplant
6. Rare diseases
7. Congenital coagulation factor abnormalities
8. Severe burns
9. Ventilator dependence
10. Others announced by the central competent authority

6.2 Application for Major Illness/Injury Card
Application requirements:
- Diagnosed by a physician as having a major illness/injury
- Requires continuous treatment

Application process:
1. Ask the physician to issue a diagnostic certificate
2. Fill out the major illness/injury application form
3. Attach relevant examination reports
4. Submit to the NHIA for review
5. A major illness/injury certificate will be issued upon approval

6.3 Major Illness/Injury Benefits
Benefits for holding a major illness/injury card:
- Exempt from co-payment for medical treatment
- Not limited to specific medical institutions
- No referral required
- Free continuous prescription for chronic diseases

Note: Only for medical treatment related to the major illness/injury

Chapter 7: Special Medical Treatment

7.1 Preventive Healthcare Services
Adult preventive healthcare:
- 40-64 years old: Once every 3 years
- 65 years and older: Once a year
- Polio patients over 35 years old: Once a year

Examination items:
- Physical examination
- Blood tests (blood sugar, lipids, liver and kidney function)
- Urine test
- Hepatitis B screening (once in a lifetime for ages 45-79)
- Hepatitis C screening (once in a lifetime for ages 45-79)

Cervical smear test for women:
- Women over 30 years old
- Once a year

Mammography:
- Women aged 45-69
- Once every 2 years

Colorectal cancer screening:
- People aged 50-74
- Fecal occult blood test once every 2 years

Oral cancer screening:
- Betel nut chewers or smokers over 30 years old
- Once every 2 years

Child preventive healthcare:
- Children under 7 years old
- A total of 9 free health checks

Prenatal examination for pregnant women:
- A total of 10 times during pregnancy
- 2 ultrasound examinations

7.2 Dental Treatment
NHI covered items:
- Teeth cleaning (once every six months)
- Tooth extraction
- Root canal treatment
- General filling
- Simple dentures

Partially covered items:
- Fixed dentures (co-payment required)
- Removable dentures (partially covered, e.g., for fully edentulous)

Non-covered items:
- Dental implants
- Orthodontics
- Whitening
- Veneers
- Periodontal laser treatment

7.3 Traditional Chinese Medicine Treatment
NHI covered items:
- Chinese medicine outpatient consultation
- Chinese medicine traumatology
- Acupuncture
- Chinese medicine

Notes:
- Outpatient co-payment is 50 NTD per visit
- A 28-day supply of medication can be prescribed for chronic diseases
- Can see both Chinese and Western medicine doctors simultaneously
- Some concentrated Chinese medicines require out-of-pocket payment

Chapter 8: Overseas Medical Treatment

8.1 Reimbursement for Out-of-Pocket Medical Expenses Abroad
Applicable situations:
- Sudden illness requiring medical treatment abroad
- Does not include traveling abroad for the purpose of medical treatment
- Does not include cosmetic surgery, health check-ups, etc.

Application deadline:
- Apply within 6 months after returning to Taiwan

Required documents:
1. Reimbursement application form
2. Original medical expense receipts
3. Diagnosis certificate or proof document
4. Entry and exit records for that trip
5. Chinese translation of the expense receipts

Reimbursement standard:
- Reimbursed according to the payment standard of domestic medical institutions of the same level
- Usually lower than the actual amount paid

8.2 Medical Treatment in Mainland China
Emergency medical treatment can be reimbursed:
- Limited to sudden illnesses
- Notarized documents are required
- According to the payment standards of domestic medical institutions

Long-term residence in mainland China:
- Can apply to suspend insurance
- Need to resume insurance coverage to seek medical treatment in Taiwan

Chapter 9: Complaints and Rights

9.1 Medical Dispute Resolution
Medical dispute resolution channels:
1. Communicate and coordinate with the medical institution
2. File a complaint with the hospital's complaint department
3. File a complaint with the health bureau
4. Apply for mediation
5. Legal channels

9.2 NHI Business Complaints
Complaint channels:
- NHIA service hotline: 0800-030-598
- Regional divisions of the NHIA
- Online application on the NHIA website
- Written complaint

Common complaint cases:
- Disputes over medical institution fees
- Problems with using the NHI card
- Disputes over coverage determination
- Damage to medical rights

9.3 Handling of Premium Arrears
Impact of arrears:
- Within 3 months: Suspension of insurance benefits
- Can seek medical treatment at own expense and apply for reimbursement after paying the arrears
- Accumulated arrears: Will be subject to collection

Repayment methods:
- Convenience store payment
- Financial institution payment
- NHIA counter payment
- ATM transfer
- Credit card payment

Installment payment:
- Can apply for installment payments if the arrears reach a certain amount
- Up to 24 installments

Chapter 10: Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: What if I forget to bring my NHI card?
A: You can pay for the medical treatment out-of-pocket first, and within 6 months from the date of treatment, apply for reimbursement from the NHIA with your NHI card, receipt, and diagnosis certificate.

Q2: Can I see doctors at multiple hospitals at the same time?
A: Yes, but it is better to stick to one hospital for the same disease.

Q3: Can I get my chronic disease prescription refilled in advance?
A: No, you must wait until the date of the last refill plus the number of days of medication before you can get another refill.

Q4: Can I choose my doctor during hospitalization?
A: Yes, but the designated physician fee must be paid out-of-pocket.

Q5: What if I have doubts about the diagnosis after a consultation?
A: You can seek a second medical opinion by visiting another hospital.

Q6: Can the medical records in my NHI card be deleted?
A: No, the medical records are for medical reference.

Q7: The doctor said this is an out-of-pocket item, is that reasonable?
A: NHI has coverage limits, and special examinations or materials may require out-of-pocket payment. You can ask if there are NHI-covered alternatives.

Q8: The wait at the emergency room is very long, can I complain?
A: The emergency room operates on a triage system. If your condition is not urgent, you will have to wait. It is recommended to switch to an outpatient clinic.
